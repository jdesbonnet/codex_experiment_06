# The Long Reboot - Initial Outline

## Working Title

`The Long Reboot`

## Scope

A long-form alternate-history novel following Fiona, a medical device engineer
from `2026`, as she tries to accelerate the world from `1800` toward
`1980`-class information technology within one lifetime.

## Suggested Part Structure

### Part I - Arrival

- Chapter 1: Fiona arrives in `1800` Britain with her luggage
- Chapter 2: first shelter, first lies, first truth
- Chapter 3: patronage or prison

### Part II - Precision

- machine tools
- measurement
- workshop discipline
- chemistry and batteries
- the first telegraph demonstrations

### Part III - Wire

- telegraph network
- state interest
- commercial adoption
- military capture pressure
- Fiona learns the price of being believed

### Part IV - Fire in Glass

- vacuum work
- lamps, tubes, amplifiers
- radio
- the first central computing ideas

### Part V - Memory

- magnetic recording
- tape
- switching and routing
- institutional computing

### Part VI - The Green Window

- terminals
- message networks
- international exchange
- early electronic society

### Part VII - Ambiguous Future

- a world recognizably transformed
- Fiona older, influential, and uncertain
- success measured in capability, not moral clarity

## Narrative Approach

- close third-person following Fiona
- enough technical detail to feel plausible
- use historical figures sparingly and purposefully
- allow institutions and ordinary workers to matter, not only great inventors

## First Historical Anchors

- Napoleonic Britain
- London scientific circles
- Royal Institution orbit
- emerging industrial towns

## Immediate Drafting Rule

Each chapter should:

- work as a spoken-text unit
- end with forward motion
- balance material reality, human stakes, and technical consequence
